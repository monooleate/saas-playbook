# 19 — Discovery & Validation

> **Status:** Outline only. Full content scheduled for v0.2.
> **When to implement:** **Before any code**. Then again at every major
> feature decision.
> **Cost:** 1–4 weeks for serious validation; ongoing.
> **Belongs to:** *Pre-foundations*. Numerically last but logically first.

## Scope

What you do *before* writing the first line of product code:

1. **Problem validation** — does the pain you imagine actually exist
   for the people you imagine?
2. **Customer interviews** — talking to 10–30 prospects with a
   structured script.
3. **Pre-MVP signals** — landing pages, email-list waitlists, fake-
   door tests, smoke tests.
4. **Competitive analysis** — what already exists, why it isn't
   enough, where you'll insert.
5. **MVP scope** — the smallest product that lets one customer pay you
   without you being embarrassed.
6. **Killing the idea** — explicit kill criteria. The discipline of
   walking away.
7. **Re-validation** — every quarter, every major pivot.

## Why it matters

Solo founders ship the wrong product faster than anyone else. The
checklist below is not romantic; it is the difference between
shipping at month 3 to crickets vs. month 4 to ten paying customers.

The honest data: most failed SaaS started with a product that had no
buyers, not with a buyer base that had no product.

## Patterns

### The Mom Test

Rob Fitzpatrick's [The Mom Test](https://www.momtestbook.com/) is the
canonical reference. Three rules for customer interviews:
1. Talk about their life, not your idea.
2. Ask about specifics in the past, not generics or opinions about the
   future.
3. Talk less, listen more.

If you do nothing else from this topic, read this book.

### Smoke test landing page

Cheapest validation: a landing page with a clear value prop, screenshot
mockups, a "Sign up" button that goes to a "Coming soon — leave your
email" form. Drive ~$50 of paid traffic. Watch the conversion.

- Conversion < 1% → message or audience is wrong.
- Conversion 1–3% → there's something; iterate.
- Conversion > 3% → you have signal; build.

### Pre-orders / pre-payments

The strongest signal possible: someone gives you money before the
product exists. Stripe Payment Links lets you do this in 5 minutes.

### Kill criteria

Define before you start: "If by week 12 I do not have 3 paying customers
and a clear path to 10, I will pivot or stop."
