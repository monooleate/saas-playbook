# 24 — Launch Playbook

> **Status:** Outline only. Full content scheduled for v0.2.
> **When to implement:** Roughly 2–3 weeks before public launch; runs
> for ~30 days.
> **Cost:** 5–10 days, concentrated.
> **Belongs to:** *Operations* (one-time, high-leverage).

## Scope

The one-time event sequence around making a product publicly available:

1. **Beta program** — pre-launch closed group, structured feedback.
2. **Pre-launch waitlist** — building anticipation; converting on
   launch day.
3. **Launch checklist** — everything that must work on launch day
   (cross-references topics 01–23).
4. **Product Hunt launch** — preparation, day-of mechanics, follow-up.
5. **Hacker News / Reddit / X launch** — channel-specific tactics.
6. **Press / outreach** — bloggers, newsletters, podcasts in the
   niche.
7. **Day-1, week-1, month-1 metrics** — what you measure and how to
   react.
8. **Post-launch retro** — what worked, what didn't.

## Why it matters

Launch day is a one-shot acquisition event. A solo SaaS may get one
real "launch moment" that the marketing site can point to forever
("Featured on Product Hunt, #2 of the day"). Wasted = wasted forever;
nailed = compounding distribution.

That said: launches are *not* the only path. Many successful solo
SaaS skip the "launch day" theatrics and grow via SEO + content. This
topic covers launch tactics for those who choose them; topic 17
(Marketing & SEO) covers the alternative.

## The 30/14/7/1/0 timeline

- **T-30 days** — beta wraps. All major bugs fixed. Marketing site
  final. Pricing page live.
- **T-14 days** — Product Hunt scheduled. Pre-launch posts go out
  on X/LinkedIn. Email outreach to early supporters.
- **T-7 days** — final dry run: signup, payment, cancel, support.
  All systems verified.
- **T-1 day** — assets prepared (Tweet thread, PH gallery, email
  draft to waitlist).
- **T-0 (launch day)** — PH goes live; you respond to every comment
  for 24 hours.
